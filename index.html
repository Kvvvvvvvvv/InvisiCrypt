üîç What is Steganography?

Steganography is the ancient art and science of hiding secret information within other non-secret data or physical objects. The term comes from the Greek words "steganos" (covered) and "graphein" (writing), literally meaning "covered writing."

üéØ How It Works

InvisiCrypt uses advanced LSB (Least Significant Bit) steganography to embed secret messages within image pixels. Each pixel's color channels are subtly modified to store binary data, making the changes imperceptible to the human eye.

üî¨ Technical Details

Our algorithm modifies the least significant bits of RGB color channels, allowing up to 3 bits of data per pixel. This provides excellent capacity while maintaining image quality and avoiding detection.
